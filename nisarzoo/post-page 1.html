Horse

The domesticated horse (Equus ferus caballus)[2][3] is a hoofed, one-toed mammal. It is one of the two existing subspecies of Equus ferus and a member of the taxonomic family Equidae. The horse has changed from a small, multi-toed animal that was related to Eohippus to a gigantic, single-toed mammal over the previous 45 to 55 million years. Horse domestication is said to have started around 4000 BCE and expanded significantly by 3000 BCE. The caballus subspecies of horses are domesticated, yet certain populations of domesticated horses roam freely as wild horses. Since the term "wild horses" only applies to animals that have never been domesticated, these feral populations are not actually wild horses.
